Rule of Thumb
Dev gets tagged on anything requiring a code fix or platform-side investigation. Alec gets looped when it is client-facing severity — Critical, service disruption, compliance, or any escalated case past SLA. Joe and Jose are the destination for Feature Requests only — not active triage participants.
Section 4

SLA Best Practices

Transform does not currently have automated SLA breach logic. The targets below represent best practice for a team of this size and client profile. Confirm with Alec before communicating any SLA commitments externally.

Current State
The SLA Breach checkbox is a manual flag. Until Target Resolution Date is added and auto-trigger logic is built (see Section 10), SLA monitoring requires a manual daily review using the Created At and Last Modified At fields that already exist in the form.
PriorityFirst Response TargetResolution TargetBreach = When This Is Missed
Critical2 business hoursSame business dayNo first response in 2 hrs, or not Resolved by EOD
High4 business hours1 business dayPast Target Resolution Date without Resolved/Closed status
Medium1 business day3 business daysPast Target Resolution Date without Resolved/Closed status
Low2 business days7 business daysPast Target Resolution Date without Resolved/Closed status
Cancelled Cases and SLA
Cancelled cases must be excluded from SLA breach metrics. A case that was withdrawn before work began should not be measured against response or resolution targets — including it would artificially inflate breach rates and distort reporting.

Manual SLA Monitoring — Daily Habit Until Automation Exists

  • Each morning, review all open cases using Created At and Last Modified At to identify elapsed time.
  • Any case where elapsed time since creation exceeds the resolution target for its Priority — manually check SLA Breach.
  • Any Critical or High case not moving within the first response window — contact the assigned owner directly before flagging the breach.
  • Any case with Last Modified At older than 5 business days regardless of priority — treat as stale and address proactively.
  • When a breach occurs, reach out to the client before they reach out to you.
Section 5

Awaiting Response — Rename Recommendation

The Status dropdown already includes Waiting on Customer, which captures the state where client action is needed before the case can progress. The Awaiting Response checkbox as currently named describes the same condition — making it redundant.

The recommendation is to rename the Awaiting Response checkbox to "Awaiting Internal Response." This creates a meaningful distinction between two different blocked states that the current form cannot differentiate.

Status: Waiting on Customer

  • Progress is blocked on the client
  • A follow-up has been sent and a client response is required
  • Already exists in the Status dropdown — no change needed
  • Unset when: client responds, status moves to In Progress

Checkbox: Awaiting Internal Response (renamed)

  • Progress is blocked on an internal teammate — Dev or Alec
  • A question or request has been sent internally and a response is needed
  • Field rename only — no logic change required
  • Unset when: internal teammate responds, work resumes
Usage Rule
A case can be both Waiting on Customer (status) and Awaiting Internal Response (checkbox) simultaneously — waiting for the client to confirm AND waiting for Dev to validate a fix. They are independent signals, not alternatives.
Enhancement Request
Log a request to Jose to rename the "Awaiting Response" checkbox to "Awaiting Internal Response." Field rename only — no logic change required. See Section 10.
Section 6

Escalation Field Best Practices

The three escalation fields — Status = Escalated, the Escalated checkbox, and Escalated To — are a compound action. All three fire together or none of them do.

Hard Rule
A case should never have the Escalated checkbox checked without Escalated To populated and Status set to Escalated. Any partial state creates ambiguity about whether the case is truly escalated and being actively managed.

The Escalation Checklist

  • Set Status = Escalated
  • Check the Escalated checkbox
  • Populate Escalated To with a specific teammate — never a team or queue
  • Populate Escalation Notes: what is blocked, what the escalated person needs to do, and the urgency level
  • Set Escalation Date manually at the time of escalation
  • Notify the escalated person directly via Slack — do not rely on the platform to alert them

When to Escalate

  • Any Critical or High case not resolved within the SLA target
  • Any case requiring a code fix or platform-side investigation (→ Dev)
  • Any case involving data integrity, security, or compliance (→ Alec)
  • Any case where the client has expressed urgency beyond normal channels
  • Any case not progressing after 5+ business days with no clear resolution path
  • Any Incident or Service Disruption affecting multiple clients

De-escalation Rules

  • When Dev or Alec resolves their piece, update Status back to In Progress
  • Uncheck the Escalated checkbox
  • Add a note to Escalation Notes documenting what was resolved and by whom
  • Reassign to the appropriate owner to complete resolution
  • Do not close a case while the Escalated checkbox is still checked — de-escalate first
Section 7

Operational Best Practices

Triage

TDaily triage habit

Build a morning check of all New cases. Any case in New status overnight without being triaged is a risk. Target: all New cases triaged within 24 hours of submission.

5The 5-field minimum

Account, Priority, Type, Assignee, and Team/Queue must all be populated before a case leaves Triaged status. If any are missing, the case is reviewed — not triaged.

→Triage is not a holding state

A case should not remain in Triaged status for more than 1 business day. If it does, the assigned owner has not acknowledged or begun work.

!Critical bypass

Critical and High cases bypass normal queue order — alert Dev and/or Alec immediately, then complete triage fields. Speed of first response takes priority over perfect classification.

Resolution Notes

RRequired to close

Resolution Notes must be populated before any case moves to Resolved or Closed. This is a standing operational rule until it can be enforced by platform logic (see Section 10).

KWhat a good note includes

(1) Root cause. (2) What was done to fix it. (3) Client communication or verification. Over time these become searchable answers for recurring issues.
Good vs. Bad Resolution Note
Bad: "Fixed." or "Resolved by Dev." — No root cause, no reuse value.

Good: "User lacked the correct role in Transform. Added CX Viewer role to their account. Walked client through verification on call 3/15. Root cause: roles not provisioned during initial onboarding. Added to onboarding checklist."

Feature Request Handling

FNever let them die in the queue

Feature Requests must be logged in the product backlog with Jose before closing the case. Closing the case does not mean the request was heard.

+Capture context, not just the ask

Log: client name, specific use case, business impact, and how many accounts have made the same request. Volume signals priority to product.

3The 3-account rule

If the same request appears from 3 or more accounts, bring it to Joe and Jose immediately — do not wait for the next product cadence meeting.

↑Bring to product cadence

Create a standing agenda item with Joe and Jose. The Feature Request log is CX's most direct product influence lever — bring it every time.

Triage vs. Resolution

TriageResolution
PurposeClassify, prioritize, and route the caseInvestigate, fix, communicate, and close the case
OwnerAlways Tracey — triage belongs to CX regardless of where the case routes nextAssigned owner (CX, Dev, or both)
Duration5–15 minutes per caseHours to days depending on complexity
What happensAccount, Priority, Type, Assignee, Team/Queue set. Target Resolution Date set. Alerts sent if Critical/High.Work performed. Client updated. Notes captured throughout. Resolution Notes required before close.
Status resultNew → Triaged → In Progress (within 1 business day)In Progress → Resolved → Closed
Failure modeTriage becomes a holding state — cases sit indefinitely with no active ownerCase closes without Resolution Notes, leaving no institutional memory
Key Distinction
Triage answers: What is this, who owns it, and how urgent is it?
Resolution answers: What was wrong, what fixed it, and is the client satisfied?

Closed vs. Cancelled
Closed means the case reached a resolution — work was done, the client was satisfied, and Resolution Notes exist.

Cancelled means the case was withdrawn before resolution — no fix was needed or delivered. Using Closed for unworked cases pollutes resolution metrics and makes reporting unreliable.
Section 10

Enhancement Requests for Jose

The items below represent platform improvements that would reduce manual overhead and enforce operational consistency. These are not current capabilities — they are proposed enhancements to be logged and prioritized with Jose. Each can be submitted as a Feature Request/Enhancement case type.

Submission Note
Log each item as a separate Feature Request/Enhancement case so they can be individually prioritized. Lead with the three quick wins: the checkbox rename, and the two saved views — those require no logic changes and deliver immediate value.
  • Rename "Awaiting Response" to "Awaiting Internal Response" — field rename only, no logic change. Eliminates overlap with the Waiting on Customer status.
  • Add Cancelled as a Status option — new terminal status value alongside Closed. Distinct meaning: case withdrawn before resolution. No Resolution Notes required.
  • Cancellation Reason field — conditionally required when Status = Cancelled — dropdown with values: Submitted in error · Duplicate · Client withdrew request · Issue resolved itself · Out of scope · Other (requires brief note). Conditional display and required behavior requires platform logic.
  • Exclude Cancelled cases from SLA Breach reporting — cases with Status = Cancelled should not be evaluated against first response or resolution targets.
  • Auto-set Escalation Date when Escalated checkbox is checked — removes manual date entry and ensures escalation timing is always captured.
  • Require Resolution Notes before Resolved or Closed status is selectable — form validation that prevents closure without documentation. Does not apply to Cancelled.
  • Auto-trigger SLA Breach checkbox when Target Resolution Date has passed and status is not Resolved, Closed, or Cancelled — eliminates daily manual breach monitoring.
  • Conditional required field: Steps to Reproduce for Bug/Defect and Integration Issue types — ensures Dev receives reproduction steps on every relevant case.
  • Account / Company field linked to a client master list — dropdown from an account registry rather than free text, enabling consistent naming and account-level reporting.
  • Auto-set Team/Queue based on Case Type + Priority routing rules — reduces manual routing and enforces consistent assignment logic.
  • Saved view: Cases past SLA target — open cases (excluding Cancelled) where time elapsed since Created At exceeds the resolution target for their Priority. For daily manual monitoring until auto-trigger is built.
  • Saved view: Cases not updated in 5+ business days — filters on Last Modified At relative to today, excluding Cancelled and Closed. Surfaces stale cases before they become client escalations.
